What is a Rover?

The rover, pictured below is a digital rendering of a the curiosity, is a robot sent to mars to collet data through pictures and sometimes rock samples. You can see from the diagram below that the rover has a lot of cameras. The different cameras have unique functions. Some are used for navigation. Some are used to magnify the surface, so it can be studied at greater detail, and others have sensors to detect hazards, so the rover can avoid them. The rover is very expensive, and it is on Mars, so if it were damaged, it is impossible to fix, so avoiding hazards is very important. All of the pictures the rover takes are sent to NASA to be studied by scientists, but they are also accesible to the public, if you want to find them. Luckily, we have access to these photos, so we can study them to help us plan our colony for Mr. Tusk.
